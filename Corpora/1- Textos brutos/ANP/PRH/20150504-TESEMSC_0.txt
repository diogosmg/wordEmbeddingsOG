REVERSE TIME MIGRATION IN ISOTROPIC TRANSVERSELY MEDIA

Carlos Henrique dos Santos Barbosa

March/2015

Advisor: Luiz Landau

Department: Civil Engineering

The imaging of complex geological structures is a very challenging problem in the
hydrocarbon industry. There are several techniques that help geoscientists under-
stand how geological layers are arranged in subsurface. Among them, the Reverse
Time Migration has an important role because it is based on two-way wave equa-
tion, and this feature allows imaging the reﬂectors with no dip limitation besides
considering the extreme lateral velocity variation. In this work, it was studied how
the imaging of geological structures behaves in Transversely Isotropic media. Three
migration algorithms were implemented in order to evaluate the diﬀerences in the
migrated sections, the Reverse Time Migration based on acoustic isotropic wave
equation (RTM isotropic), the Reverse Time Migration based on pseudo-acoustic
vertical transversely isotropic wave equation (RTM VTI), and the Reverse Time Mi-
gration based on pseudo-acoustic tilted transversely isotropic wave equation (RTM
TTI). These algorithms were applied in two synthetic cases whose geology is a Tilted
Transversely Isotropic media. Therefore, it is discussed and shown the low resolution
and misplaced images of subsurface structures due to isotropic, and VTI assumption.
Finally, the dataset BP 2007 TTI 2-D provided by courtesy of BP Exploration Op-
eration Company Limited was migrated with the techniques RTM Isotropic, RTM
VTI, and RTM TTI. In addition, the Common Image Gathers (CIGs) were compared
in order to evaluate if the events are ﬂat.
